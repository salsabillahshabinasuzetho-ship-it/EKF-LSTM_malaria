# EKF-LSTM_malaria

Accurate multi-step forecasting of malaria dynamics remains a challenging task due to the nonlinear interactions between human and mosquito populations and the limited observability of epidemiological states. This study proposes a state-estimation-based forecasting framework for predicting malaria transmission dynamics using a human–mosquito SEIR–SEI mathematical model. EKF is applied for simultaneous state and parameter estimation in a seven-compartment model consisting of $S_h$, $E_h$, $I_h$, $R_h$, $S_m$, $E_m$, and $I_m$, while LSTM is used for multi-step forecasting. The results show that EKF accurately estimates the model dynamics, indicated by a low RMSE of 0.0022. In addition, LSTM achieves satisfactory predictive performance with a MAPE of 9.92\%, RMSE of 42{,}132.58, MAE of 33{,}832.03, and $R^2$ of 0.8507. The forecasting performance shows satisfactory accuracy with a MAPE of 9.92\%, RMSE of 42,132.58, MAE of 33,832.03, and R² of 0.8507. Forecast simulations indicate an increasing trend in malaria cases, rising from approximately 541,824 cases in 2025 to 809,787 cases in 2029. These findings demonstrate that the proposed state-estimation-based forecasting framework provides an effective tool for predicting malaria transmission dynamics and supports data-driven decision-making for malaria prevention and control programs.
